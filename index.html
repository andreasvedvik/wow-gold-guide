⚔ Gold-Making Fundamentals

The stuff that actually matters — ignore this and you'll stay broke

📊 Don't Put All Your Gold in One Bag

If you're only doing one thing to make gold, you're one hotfix away from being broke. Markets crash, farms get nerfed, and that herb that was 50g yesterday is 5g today. Spread your gold-making across a few different methods so when Blizz inevitably nerfs your favorite farm, you've got backup.

⏱ Gold Per Hour

Seriously, track your GPH. If you're flying loops for 2 hours and earning less than you could from other methods, switch strategies. Every minute spent farming is a minute not spent on something more profitable. Time is your most finite resource — don't waste it on low-GPH activities.

📅 Timing Is Everything

Tuesday reset is the highest-demand day for consumables and gear. Raid groups panic-buy flasks, potions, enchants, and repairs. Prices spike hard on Tuesday-Wednesday. Gather early in the week when supply is abundant and prices are low, then sell everything into the Tuesday demand spike. This timing pattern repeats every single week.

🧠 Knowledge = Gold

This one separates the rich from the broke. Spend a week just watching your server. Where do prices go? When do they tank? The AH rewards patients and punishes panic. Don't be impulsive.

🆕 New Player Basics

Foundation concepts that power all gold-making strategies

🏦 The Auction House (AH)

The AH is the central marketplace for trading items. Find an Auctioneer NPC (gavel icon on your map) to list items for sale or browse listings. When your item sells, Blizzard takes a 5% cut and you pocket the rest. Commodities (herbs, ore, cloth) trade on a region-wide AH with massive volume and competition. Crafted gear is server-specific, so competition is localized. Understanding which market you're competing in is essential for pricing.

📅 What's "Reset Day"?

Reset day is Tuesday when mythic+ and raid instances reset, and guilds prepare for raid night. Suddenly there's massive demand for flasks, potions, enchants, repairs, and stat food. Prices spike hard on Tuesday and stay elevated through Wednesday. By the weekend, casual players have farmed everything to death and supply crashes prices. Understanding this weekly cycle is more valuable than any other single pattern — it applies to basically every profitable item.

⚒ Profession Slots & Pairing

Each character gets two primary profession slots and unlimited secondary slots. Primary professions: choose two from Herbalism, Mining, Skinning, Alchemy, Enchanting, Blacksmithing, Jewelcrafting, Tailoring, Inscription, Leatherworking, Engineering. Secondary professions (Fishing, Cooking, Alchemy counts as both) can be done on every character without taking slots. Smart pairing: Herbalism + Alchemy (gather herbs, craft potions), Mining + Jewelcrafting (gather ore, cut gems), or pair any gathering with a crafting profession.

📊 Gathering Stats Explained

Perception increases your chance of rare procs like Nocturnal Lotus — stack this early for maximum profit on rare drops. Finesse increases the quantity of materials you gather per node and chance of higher-quality gathers — stack this for volume farming. Deftness speeds up gather time slightly. Priority: Perception first (big profit multiplier), then Finesse for grinding efficiency.

🔧 How to Install Addons

Download CurseForge desktop app, search for addon names (TradeSkillMaster, CraftSim, Routes, GatherMate2, Auctionator), click install. WoW automatically loads them on login. These aren't cheating — they're information tools that level the playing field. Addon-less gold-making is viable but handicaps you significantly compared to experienced players. Install the essentials before you start farming.

🏦 Warband Bank & Cross-Character Gold Pooling

The Warband Bank is shared storage accessible from all your characters. Use it to move materials and gold between toons. Strategy: gather on your main, send materials to a dedicated crafter alt, then consolidate all gold into your banking alt. This lets you scale up farming (main stays on gathering instead of selling), and you accumulate capital for bigger flipping operations. Without Warband Bank, each character's economy is isolated — with it, you're managing an account-wide operation.

⛏ Gathering Professions

The most accessible and reliable gold sources — no startup cost, no knowledge investment needed to begin

Supply & Demand

Blizzard's restrictions on multiboxing and bot enforcement have significantly reduced automated supply flooding the market. This means solo gatherers now enjoy better prices and less competition than they did in previous expansions. Gathering profitability is genuinely high because bot supply is down and legitimate demand is up.

🌿 Herbalism

▼

Herbalism is one of the most reliable earners in every expansion. Raiding guilds burn through hundreds of flasks and potions weekly, which means herbs are always in demand. In Midnight, all six herbs spawn in every zone, so your route choice comes down to which mote type you want.

Route Strategy

All herbs spawn in every Midnight zone, so pick your route based on mote type rather than herb type. Harandar has the densest node spawns overall. Stack Perception on your profession gear for more Nocturnal Lotus procs — these are worth significantly more per unit than common herbs.

Mounted Gathering

Herbalism has a specialization node that lets you gather while mounted — no dismounting required. This is a massive GPH increase. Prioritize unlocking it early in your Botany specialization tree. Druids with flight-form gathering have a natural advantage here, making them the best herbalism class by far.

Quality Tiers

Gathered herbs use a simplified 2-tier quality system (Tier 1 and Tier 2). Higher Finesse and Perception stats increase your chance of gathering Tier 2 materials, which sell for significantly more on the AH and are required for high-rank crafts. Prioritize Perception early for rare procs, then Finesse for volume.

⚠ Bot Activity

Some servers experience heavy bot activity that craters herb prices. If your server's herb prices are unusually low, consider farming during off-peak hours, selling into Tuesday reset demand when prices spike regardless, or switching to a different gold method until prices recover.

Since Fishing is a secondary profession, every character can learn it without using a profession slot — run it on all your alts for multiplied output.

Lure System

Midnight introduces crafted lures that attract specific rare fish from pools. Lures are made by Engineers and Alchemists — buy or craft them to target high-value catches. 🔨 Crafting Professions

Higher investment, higher reward — crafting professions are the backbone of the WoW economy

Concentration — Key to Profits

Concentration is a limited resource that guarantees a higher quality result on any craft. It regenerates slowly over time across your account. The profit difference between a Rank 4 and Rank 5 craft can be 10x or more — use Concentration strategically on high-value crafts where the quality jump has the biggest price impact. Never waste it on low-margin items. This single mechanic separates profitable crafters from break-even ones.

🧪 Alchemy

The most reliably profitable crafting profession across every expansion. Flasks, potions, and cauldrons are consumed by every raider and M+ player weekly, creating constant demand. Alchemy also provides housing dyes for the decor market and has unique proc mechanics that generate free bonus items on crafts.

  • Flasks and phials for raiders (constant demand)
  • Combat potions (used in M+ and raids)
  • Cauldrons for guild groups (high material cost, high profit margin)
  • Transmutations for daily cooldown profit
  • Housing dyes for recoloring decor items (new Midnight niche)
  • Treasure-finding potions (if available) — a goblin staple that increases gold/material drops from mobs. Check Wowhead for the Midnight equivalent at launch

Spec Trees: Alchemical Mastery, Potioncraft, Phial Mastery, Transmutation

Alchemy Procs & Mixology

Alchemists have a chance to proc extra items when crafting flasks and potions — this is a significant part of why Alchemy is Tier S. The Mixology passive also doubles your own flask duration, saving gold on personal consumables. Both effects scale with your specialization investment, so deeper spec = more free items over time.

Tier S — Best consistent earner
✨ Enchanting

Enchanters profit from two angles: selling weapon and armor enchants (always in demand when players get new gear), and crafting Enchanted Crests used for upgrading high-tier gear. Mana Oils provide another steady revenue stream. Disenchanting cheap crafted items for materials is also a reliable source of enchanting reagents.

  • Weapon enchants (big seller every reset)
  • Enchanted Crests for gear upgrades
  • Mana Oils for temporary weapon buffs
  • Disenchanting cheap gear for materials

Spec Trees: Enchantment, Insight of Destruction, Insight of the Siphon, Artistry

Tier S — Dual income streams
⚒ Blacksmithing

High-end crafted plate armor and weapons command top gold through crafting orders. Requires significant Knowledge Point investment to reach max rank, but the per-item profit is substantial once you get there. Alloy conversions provide a daily cooldown income source while you build toward max rank.

  • Crafted weapons and plate armor (crafting orders)
  • Alloy conversions for daily profit
  • Sharpening stones and weapon enhancements
  • Housing decor (metal furniture, weapon racks, anvils)

Spec Trees: Armorsmithing, Weaponsmithing, Smelting, Hammercraft

Tier A — High per-item profit
💎 Jewelcrafting

Nearly all gear can have gem sockets in Midnight, making Jewelcrafting relevant for the entire playerbase. Cut gems sell in massive volume, while JC-exclusive embellishments and crafted jewelry provide high-ticket crafting order income. Prospecting ore for rare gems adds a gambling element with potentially huge payoffs.

  • Cut gems for every gear slot (massive volume)
  • JC-exclusive embellishments
  • Prospecting ore for rare gems
  • Crafted jewelry via crafting orders
  • Housing decor (gem-studded items, crystal displays, chandeliers)

Spec Trees: Faceting, Jewelcraft, Shaping, Gemology

Tier A — High volume sales
🧵 Tailoring

A major change in Midnight: only characters with Tailoring can loot cloth from mobs. This means the entire cloth supply comes from Tailors, driving up prices significantly. Tailoring functions as both a gathering and crafting profession — sell raw cloth for easy gold, or craft it into armor, bags, and spellthreads for higher margins.

  • Exclusive cloth drops (sell raw or craft)
  • Crafted cloth armor and embellishments
  • Spellthread leg enchants
  • Bags (evergreen market)
  • Housing decor (tapestries, curtains, cloth furnishings)

Spec Trees: Tailorcraft, Clothcraft, Garmentcraft, Fabrication

Tier A — Unique cloth monopoly
📜 Inscription

Scribes produce Vantus Runes (weekly raid buff items that sell well on reset day), Missives for stat customization on crafted gear, and Darkmoon trinket cards. The real passive income comes from Treatises — every crafter in the game needs one per profession per week for their +1 Knowledge Point, creating enormous and consistent demand.

  • Vantus Runes (weekly raid buff items)
  • Missives for stat customization
  • Treatises — weekly KP items every crafter needs (+1 Knowledge Point per profession per week). Guaranteed passive sales
  • Contracts for reputation gains
  • Darkmoon trinket cards
  • Housing decor items (scrolls, paintings, banners) and contracts

Spec Trees: Darkmoon Sigils, Archiving, Pursuit of Knowledge, Rune Mastery

Tier B — Niche but steady
🎖 Leatherworking

Leatherworkers craft leather and mail armor for a wide range of classes. High-rank crafting orders provide solid per-item profit, armor kits are always in demand as gear enhancements, and the housing decor market (leather furniture, mounted trophies, rugs) adds a new revenue stream.

  • Crafted leather and mail armor (crafting orders)
  • Armor kits for gear enhancements
  • Specialized bags and containers
  • Housing decor (leather furniture, rugs, banners)

Spec Trees: Leathercraft, Mailcraft, Tanning, Curing

Tier A — Steady crafting order income
⚙ Engineering

Engineering's core gold loop in Midnight revolves around Evercores — recycle random reagents into these, then use them to craft gear, gadgets, and utility items. The recycling system means Engineers can turn cheap surplus materials from other professions into valuable crafting components. Lures, bombs, and overcharged gear provide additional income.

  • Reagent recycling into Evercores (the core Engineering loop)
  • Crafted engineered gear — goggles, guns, and gadgets
  • Utility items (Overcharged gear, lures, bombs)
  • Housing decor items (mechanical furniture, lights)

Spec Trees: Explosives, Gear, Gadgetry, Mechanical Forging

Tier A — Evercore recycling has potential
🍳 Cooking

Often overlooked but highly profitable. Raid feasts and stat food are consumed by the hundreds every week by serious guilds. Since Cooking is a secondary profession, every character can learn it without using a profession slot. The key to profit is self-supplying fish through Fishing — buying materials at AH prices cuts deeply into feast margins.

  • Raid feasts (the highest-demand consumable in the game)
  • Individual stat food for M+ and PvP
  • Haste/Crit/Vers food for different specs
  • Low barrier to entry — no profession slot needed

Secondary profession — everyone can learn it. Pairs with Fishing for self-supplied materials.

Margin Warning

Feast profit margins depend heavily on your material source. If you're buying fish at full AH prices, margins can be razor thin or even negative. Self-supplying through Fishing eliminates material costs and turns Cooking into a high-margin profession. Consider pairing Fishing + Cooking on a dedicated alt for maximum efficiency.

Tier A — Consistent weekly feast demand

🌞 Eversong Woods

Easiest zone, tons of herbs. Follow the rivers. This is where newbies should farm.

Route Strategy

Follow the rivers in a large loop. Herb and ore nodes spawn densely along waterways. Use Rich Soil to plant seeds for bonus herbs. This zone is ideal for dual Herb/Mining characters.

🚀 15k–25k GPH (week 1) · 📉 6k–12k GPH (steady state)
🐯 Zul'Aman

Hug the edges to avoid elevation hell. Wild Overload gives you stuff to kill for extra herbs. Good for rare herb types.

Route Strategy

Hug the outer edge in a full loop. Wild Overload gives a buff for additional rare materials, and lashers from wild nodes yield bonus herbs. One of the best zones for mote farming if specced into Overload.

🚀 12k–22k GPH (week 1) · 📉 5k–10k GPH (steady state)
🏔 Harandar — Herb-Rich Farming

Most herbs of any zone. Figure-eight loop in the middle. Pure volume play.

Route Strategy

Two interlocking loops through the central valleys. Highest node density of any zone. Best for pure herb farming without Overload spec. Primal Overload: channel 50% HP for extra motes — don't interrupt the cast!

🚀 14k–24k GPH (week 1) · 📉 6k–12k GPH (steady state)
💜 Voidstorm — Mote Farming

Janky terrain but best mote spawns. Dodge mobs. Best Overload in the game but high risk.

Route Strategy

Follow ridgeline paths to avoid mobs. Best for short 10-15 min bursts rather than long sessions. Voidbound Overload is the highest-reward overload type: portal spawns 4 orbs for extra motes and herbs. Campaign completion required for zone access.

⚠ Warning

Voidstorm has the highest mob density of any gathering zone. Bring self-healing or a tanky spec. Leave fight-heavy areas immediately — dead time kills GPH.

🚀 10k–18k GPH (week 1) · 📉 4k–9k GPH (steady state)

💨 Speed Set Guide

Gear optimization for clearing legacy content faster with higher GPH potential

⚡ Why You Need a Speed Set

Move at 240% speed and you'll clear Motherlode in 2 minutes instead of 5. Over 10 runs, that's 30 minutes saved. That's money.

Note: Building a speed set is a mid-to-long-term investment. If you're a brand new player with one character and limited gold, focus on gathering and basic gold-making first — come back to this once you have 50k+ gold and are ready to optimize your legacy farming.

🐱 Best Class: Feral Druid

Feral Druids dominate speed farming because Travel Form gives instant mounted speed without dismounting, and Cat Form stealth lets you skip mobs entirely. This combination is unbeatable for legacy dungeon clear speed. Other strong options include Demon Hunter (double jump + Fel Rush for terrain skipping), Monk (Roll spam), and Hunter (Aspect of the Cheetah sprint).

Runner-ups: Demon Hunter (double jump + Fel Rush), Monk (Roll), Hunter (Aspect of the Cheetah)

🏃 Best Races

Horde: Zandalari Trolls get a permanent +5% movement speed racial that stacks with all other speed bonuses. This small multiplier adds up significantly over long farming sessions. Pick this for maximum GPH optimization.

Alliance: Night Elves can shadowmeld to stealth through mobs and skip entire dungeon sections, saving significant time. This is more valuable than raw speed on certain routes. Pick Night Elf for Alliance-side farming.

🛡 Core Gear: Azerite Armor + Longstrider

The backbone of every speed set is BFA Azerite Armor with the Longstrider trait. This gives a permanent ~25% movement speed boost based on your highest secondary stat. You need three pieces (head, shoulders, chest) plus the Heart of Azeroth neck to activate the traits. Farm these on Normal mode only — normal versions give higher Longstrider values.

🛒 Filling Remaining Slots

For every slot not covered by Azerite gear, buy cheap BoE greens from the AH that have the +Speed tertiary stat. Use Undermine Exchange to search for level 80 items with Speed — filter by armor type and sort by price. Stack Critical Strike as your primary secondary stat across all pieces. For boots specifically, you need legacy boots that accept the Minor Speed enchant — The Sentinel's Eternal Refuge (~200–250k) is best-in-slot, or Shadebound Treads as a budget option.

Does It Pay For Itself?

A speed set costs 50–300k to assemble depending on boot choice, but pays for itself within a week of legacy farming. The Motherlode alone at 10 runs/hour with a speed set generates 10–17k GPH in pure vendor gold. Add in transmog drops from weekly raid clears and you're looking at 50k+ per week across alts — all while half-watching Netflix.

📈 Auction House Flipping

The highest ceiling gold method — for those with capital and patience

AH flipping is the art of buying items below market value and reselling at a profit. Unlike farming, it doesn't require killing mobs or leveling professions — but it demands market knowledge, starting capital, and the discipline to be patient. Top "goblins" make millions per week through flipping alone.

📊 Getting Started with Flipping

▼

Step 1: Build capital. You need at least 50k–100k gold to start flipping effectively. Use gathering or dungeon farms to build your starting fund.

Step 2: Install TSM. TradeSkillMaster is non-negotiable for serious AH work. Set up pricing operations, sniper scans, and shopping lists.

Step 3: Learn your markets. Spend a full week just watching prices. Note daily patterns, reset-day spikes, and which items have the widest price swings.

Step 4: Start small. Begin with high-volume commodities (herbs, ores, cloth) where you can buy in bulk at low points and sell into demand. The margins are thin but the volume is safe.

Understanding Market Walls

A "wall" is a massive stack of listings at a single price point, often placed intentionally by a major player to control the market. If a wall appears at a price you know is too high, either buy through it to break it (forcing the seller to relist lower) or wait for natural demand to sell through it. Walls at genuinely underpriced levels should be bought aggressively — they're profit opportunities.

🔁 Cancel Scanning — Staying on Top

▼

AH listings are sorted by price (lowest first), and at the same price, oldest listing appears first. The first listing in the stack sells fastest. If someone undercuts you, you move to the back and sales dry up. Cancel scanning means regularly canceling your listings and relisting them to reset your position to the front. This maximizes sales velocity on high-volume items.

TSM's Cancel Scan Feature: TSM can automate this entire process. Set up a cancel scan operation and TSM automatically cancels your oldest listings and relists them fresh every hour. This keeps you at the front without manual effort. It's essential for competitive markets.

Strategic Use: Cancel scanning is only profitable on fast-moving items (flipping rates of hours, not days). On slow items, your deposit cost will exceed the profit gain from staying at the front. Only cancel scan items that sell multiple copies per day.

⚠ Regional Competition

With commodities trading on a region-wide AH, you're competing with thousands of other flippers, not just your realm. Margins on commodities are structurally thin because volume is so massive. Instead of trying to undercut your way to profit, focus on buying at genuine lows and selling into genuine demand spikes. The winners are those who buy Saturday and sell Tuesday, not those who fight over 5-gold undercutting wars.

📋 Crafting Orders

Get paid to craft stuff

High-rank crafting produces the biggest tips in week 1 (often 50k-200k per order for rare items) because supply is limited and demand is highest. By month 2 as more crafters reach max rank, tip amounts normalize downward. Rush Knowledge Point progression on day 1 to claim the peak profits before the market stabilizes — delaying by even a few days costs you thousands in potential tips.

The system uses three order types — Public, Personal, and Guild — plus NPC Patron Orders for guaranteed weekly income. Your Artisan Moxie reputation grows as you fill orders, unlocking better Patron rewards over time.

Public Orders

Listed on the crafting order board for anyone to fill. Lower tips but higher volume. Great for leveling your skill and building Artisan Moxie. Public orders have a 30-minute completion timer once claimed — don't claim orders you can't fill quickly. If you fail to complete it, the order returns to the board and your Moxie takes a hit.

Personal Orders

Direct commissions from players who trust your skill level. Higher tips (often 5k–50k+ per craft). Build your reputation in trade chat and community discords. Personal orders also support Recrafting — upgrading existing gear quality.

Guild Orders

Fulfill orders for your guild members. Tips may be lower but volume is steady. Position yourself as the go-to crafter in your guild for reliable income. Guild orders are visible only to your guild's crafters.

Patron Orders

Weekly NPC crafting orders that provide free materials and guaranteed gold + Artisan Moxie. Complete these first every week — they're essentially free income. Higher Moxie unlocks better-paying Patron Orders over time. Check all your alts.

Building Clientele

Advertise your max-rank specializations in Trade Chat using a consistent format: list your profession, what items you can craft at max rank, and your expected tip range. Regular players looking for reliable crafters will add you to friends and come back repeatedly. Building a customer base takes work initially but generates steady personal orders that pay more than public order spam.

🔄 Material Shuffles

Multi-profession workflows that buy raw materials and sell processed outputs for profit

What Is a Shuffle?

A shuffle is a chain of profession conversions that turns cheap materials into higher-value products. Buy ore when prices are low, convert it to gems via Jewelcrafting, cut or use those gems, then sell the output. The profit comes from timing and understanding which conversions have positive margins. The most profitable shuffle changes as the economy stabilizes, so watch the community for the current meta.

Strategy Tip

Watch r/woweconomy and community guides for the meta shuffle in the first week. Don't try to discover it yourself — by the time you find it, the margins are already ruined. Copy the established shuffle immediately and execute it efficiently. First-mover advantage matters more than innovation here.

Classic Shuffle Example

Ore → Gems → Crafted Items → Disenchant

Buy ore cheap on Saturday. Smelt it, prospect for gems. Cut valuable gems or craft them into rings/jewelry. If margins are positive, sell the finished items. Alternatively, craft cheap rings and disenchant them for materials if the material price is high. Each conversion step should be profitable, or the whole shuffle fails. Always check both the forward and reverse paths.

Vendor Shuffles

Vendor shuffles occur when crafted items sell to NPCs for more gold than the raw materials cost. This is an exploit opportunity that usually appears after patches or expansion launches due to incomplete vendor pricing. For example, crafting a trinket for 2000g worth of materials that vendors for 3000g is pure profit. These shuffle opportunities don't last — Blizzard patches vendor prices within days. When you spot one, execute it ruthlessly before it's gone.

⚠ Margins Decay Fast

Once a shuffle becomes widely known, competition crushes the margins. Week 1 can yield 50%+ returns, but by week 2 competitors flood the market. If you find a good private shuffle before it goes mainstream, you're golden — but expect it to reach zero margins within days as the word spreads. This is why staying ahead of community knowledge is so valuable.

💹 Economy Tips

Principles that explain market behavior and how to capitalize on them

🌐 Region-Wide Commodity AH

Commodities (herbs, ore, cloth, etc.) are traded on a region-wide AH, not server-specific. This means massive volume and competition but also stable prices. You can't corner the market on commodities because supply is nearly infinite. Instead, focus on volume — small margins multiplied by massive sales.

📆 The Weekly Cycle

Predictable pricing patterns emerge: farmers kill mobs and gather most on weekends, flooding the market with supply Saturday-Sunday. Prices hit bottom. Prices climb through Monday-Tuesday as raiders panic-buy for raids. Reset day (Tuesday) triggers volume spike. Buy during the supply glut (Saturday), sell into demand (Tuesday). This pattern repeats every week like clockwork.

📢 Patch Day Economy

New patches create market chaos because information is scarce and demand patterns shift. Stockpile materials before a patch drops — the first wave of crafters with max recipes and materials locked in make enormous profits before competition floods in. By day 3-4, margins normalize. By week 2, the meta settles and easy money is gone.

🏠 Server Population Matters

High-population realms have more competition (more farmers, flippers, crafters) but also more buyers and volume. Low-population realms have less competition and faster list turnover, but fewer buyers means items take longer to sell. Choose your server based on your playstyle: high-pop for AH flipping, low-pop for crafting orders.

🪙 WoW Token Timing

Token prices fluctuate based on gold supply in the economy. When gold is abundant (economy crash, dupes, RMT), tokens are cheap (good time to buy game time with gold). When gold is scarce (early expansion), tokens are expensive (good time to sell gold for tokens). Track token price trends — they indicate economy health.

Golden Rule: Buy Low, Sell High

Buy Saturday when farmers flood the market with supply and prices crater. Sell Tuesday-Wednesday when raiders panic-buy for reset and prices peak. This single pattern is the foundation of 80% of gold-making strategies. Understanding the weekly cycle beats any other trick.

📖 Glossary

WoW economy and crafting terminology explained

🔑 Knowledge Points (KP)

The progression currency for profession specializations. You earn KP weekly from quests, Treatises (purchased or crafted), Patron Orders, and world treasures. The system is heavily time-gated — each profession can gain only a fixed amount of KP per week, so everyone advances at roughly the same pace regardless of playtime. This prevents paying more gold from outright skipping progression.

⭐ Artisan Moxie

Your reputation track as a crafter. Complete crafting orders and your Moxie increases, unlocking access to better-paying Patron Orders and raising the tips players will offer on commissioned crafts. Abandon or fail orders and your Moxie decreases, locking you out of high-tier work. Moxie is account-wide, so your reputation matters.

🧪 Concentration

A limited account-wide resource that guarantees a craft will produce maximum quality result. Each craft uses one Concentration. You regenerate a small number per day, but can't stockpile more than a few. Use Concentration only on high-value crafts where the quality difference (Rank 4 vs Rank 5) creates massive price variance. Wasting Concentration on low-margin items is a costly mistake.

📜 Treatises

Weekly consumable items that grant +1 Knowledge Point to a specific profession. Every player with crafting professions needs exactly one Treatise per profession per week. Since there are hundreds of crafters per realm all needing them weekly, Treatises have massive consistent demand and are one of the highest-volume items on the AH. Inscription makes them — if you're an Inscriber, this is free money every single day.

📋 Crafting Orders (Work Orders)

Player-to-crafter commissions. Players submit orders with their materials and a tip, you craft the item, they receive it. Public orders are listed for anyone, Personal orders are direct commissions. Higher Artisan Moxie unlocks better-paying Patron Orders from NPCs (guaranteed gold, provided materials). Completing orders successfully builds your reputation; abandoning or failing orders damages it. The system replaced the old crafting quest model.

🔄 Recrafting

Upgrading an existing crafted item to a higher quality tier using the same profession. Instead of crafting brand new gear, players submit their existing item plus materials for upgrade. Recrafting uses fewer materials than crafting from scratch, so players prefer it (cheaper), and crafters profit on the tip while using less resources. It's a lower-barrier entry into crafting orders for new crafters.

💎 Specialization Passives (like Unalloyed Abundance)

Profession-specific perks unlocked by investing Knowledge Points into specialization trees. Examples: Miners get free ore procs from smelting, Alchemists get bonus flask procs, Tailors get bonus cloth. These passives scale with specialization investment — deeper specs = more procs over time. They're a core part of profession profitability and why Knowledge Point allocation matters strategically.

📊 GPH (Gold Per Hour)

The primary metric for measuring gold-making efficiency. Calculate it by dividing total profit by time spent, factoring in AH cut (the 5% fee Blizzard takes from all AH sales). A farming method that generates 1,000,000 gold in 10 hours = 100k GPH. Compare GPH across different methods to optimize where you spend your time. Always include AH cut in calculations or you'll overestimate profits.
