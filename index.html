‚öî Gold-Making Fundamentals

Core principles that separate the wealthy from the broke

üìä Diversify Income

Never rely on a single gold source. Markets crash, farms get nerfed, and patches shift demand. Spread your efforts across gathering, crafting, and flipping to stay resilient.

‚è± Gold Per Hour

Track your GPH (gold per hour) for every activity. Time spent flying between nodes or waiting on AH sales counts. Always ask: "Is this the best use of my time?"

üìÖ Timing Is Everything

Prices spike on Tuesday resets and when new content drops. Consumables surge on raid nights. Gather materials early in the week and sell into Tuesday/Wednesday demand.

üß† Knowledge = Gold

Invest in learning your server's economy. Watch prices for a week before committing to a strategy. The Auction House rewards patience and punishes impulse.

üìà Auction House Flipping

The highest ceiling gold method ‚Äî especially potent during an expansion launch

AH flipping is the art of buying items below market value and reselling at a profit. Expansion launches like Midnight are the best time to flip ‚Äî prices are volatile, new players flood the AH with underpriced materials, and demand shifts daily. Top "goblins" make millions per week through flipping alone.

üìä Getting Started with Flipping

‚ñº

Step 1: Build capital. You need at least 50k‚Äì100k gold to start flipping effectively. Use gathering or dungeon farms to build your starting fund.

Step 2: Install TSM. TradeSkillMaster is non-negotiable for serious AH work. Set up pricing operations, sniper scans, and shopping lists.

Step 3: Learn your markets. Spend a full week just watching prices. Note daily patterns, reset-day spikes, and which items have the widest price swings.

Step 4: Start small. Begin with high-volume commodities (herbs, ores, cloth) where you can buy in bulk at low points and sell into demand. The margins are thin but the volume is safe.

üìã Crafting Orders

Midnight's streamlined commission system

Crafting Orders return in Midnight with a streamlined system. Fractured Sparks are gone ‚Äî whole Sparks now drop directly (with doubled costs). If you've invested knowledge points to reach max-rank crafting, this is the highest per-item gold method in the game. Build your reputation early while the market is fresh.

Public Orders

Listed on the crafting order board for anyone to fill. Lower tips but higher volume. Great for leveling your skill and building patron reputation.

Personal Orders

Direct commissions from players who trust your skill level. Higher tips (often 5k‚Äì50k+ per craft). Build your reputation in trade chat and community discords.

Guild Orders

Fulfill orders for your guild members. Tips may be lower but volume is steady. Position yourself as the go-to crafter in your guild for reliable income.

Patron Orders

Weekly NPC crafting orders that provide free materials and guaranteed income. Complete these first every week ‚Äî they're essentially free gold. Check all your alts.

Pro Tip

Advertise your max-rank specializations in Trade Chat with a clear format: profession, what you can craft at max rank, and your typical tip range. Players will add you to their friends list for repeat business. Consistency builds a customer base that pays for itself.

üîß Essential Addons

The tools every gold-maker needs installed

‚òÖ TradeSkillMaster (TSM)

The gold standard (pun intended) for AH operations. Provides real-time pricing data, automated posting/canceling, sniper scans for underpriced items, crafting cost calculations, and mail management. Steep learning curve but absolutely essential for serious gold-making.

  • Auctioning operations for automated listing
  • Sniper module for finding deals in real-time
  • Crafting module to calculate profit margins
  • Accounting to track your income over time
‚òÖ CraftSim

Handles the math behind Midnight's crafting system. Calculates the probability of reaching different quality levels (now just Silver/Gold) based on your skill, shows expected profit per craft, and helps you decide which specialization paths to invest knowledge points in.

  • Profit calculations per craft
  • Quality probability breakdowns
  • Knowledge point optimization suggestions
  • Works alongside TSM for complete coverage
‚òÖ Routes / GatherMate2

Create and share optimized gathering routes. GatherMate2 tracks node locations on your minimap, while Routes draws the most efficient path between them. Essential for maximizing GPH on gathering runs.

‚òÖ Auctionator

Lighter alternative to TSM for casual AH users. Cleaner interface for buying and selling, with shopping lists and price history. Great if TSM feels overwhelming.

üíπ Economy Tips

Understanding the meta-game of WoW's economy

üåê Region-Wide Commodity AH

All commodities (herbs, ores, cloth, consumables) sell on a region-wide AH. More buyers but more competition. At Midnight launch, prices will be chaotic for the first 1‚Äì2 weeks before stabilizing. Time your sales carefully.

üìÜ The Weekly Cycle

Prices follow a predictable weekly pattern. Materials are cheapest on weekends (more farmers online). Consumables spike Tuesday‚ÄìWednesday (raid resets). Craft and list consumables Monday night. Buy materials on Saturday.

üè† Housing Creates Permanent Demand

Unlike gear, housing decor doesn't reset with each season. This is a cosmetic market that grows over time as more players build and customize. Housing materials (especially lumber) will be in demand for months ‚Äî potentially the entire expansion.

üì¢ Expansion Launch Economy

Midnight launch is the single most profitable time to be a gold-maker. Everyone needs new enchants, gems, consumables, and crafted gear simultaneously with zero existing supply. Crafters and gatherers who are ready on day one will make a fortune.

Golden Rule

At launch, sell everything immediately ‚Äî prices are at their peak and will only go down. Once the economy stabilizes (2‚Äì3 weeks in), switch to the normal cycle: buy materials on weekends, sell consumables and enchants Tuesday through Thursday. This timing discipline alone can boost profits by 20‚Äì30%.
