⚔ Gold-Making Fundamentals

The stuff that actually matters — ignore this and you'll stay broke

📊 Don't Put All Your Gold in One Bag

If you're only doing one thing to make gold, you're one hotfix away from being broke. Markets crash, farms get nerfed, and that herb that was 50g yesterday is 5g today. Spread your gold-making across a few different methods so when Blizz inevitably nerfs your favorite farm, you've got backup.

⏱ Gold Per Hour

Seriously, track your GPH. If you're flying loops for 2 hours and making chump change, there's better stuff to do. Every minute counts.

📅 Timing Is Everything

Tuesdays are gold printing day — reset happens, raid groups panic-buy consumables, and prices go nuts. Gather early in the week, dump everything into the market Tuesday-Wednesday. Easy stonks.

🧠 Knowledge = Gold

This one separates the rich from the broke. Spend a week just watching your server. Where do prices go? When do they tank? The AH rewards patients and punishes panic. Don't be impulsive.

🆕 New Player Basics

Skip this if you've played before — read it twice if you haven't

🏦 The Auction House (AH)

It's the marketplace. Find an Auctioneer (gavel icon on the map), list stuff you wanna sell, buy stuff others are selling. Pay a 5% cut if it sells. Herbs and ore are region-wide, so you're competing with everyone. Crafted gear is server-only. That's it.

📅 What's "Reset Day"?

Tuesday resets and suddenly everyone needs flasks, pots, and enchants for raid. Prices go HARD. By weekend, casuals have farmed everything to death and it gets cheap again. If you only learn one pattern, learn this one.

⚒ Profession Limits

Two primary professions max per toon. Herbalism + Mining is brain-dead easy. If you wanna be fancy, pair gathering with crafting (Mining + Alchemy, Herbalism + Inscription). Fishing and Cooking are bonus — you can do those on any character.

📊 Gathering Stats Explained

Perception gets you rare procs. Finesse gets you more stuff per node. Deftness makes you gather faster. Get Perception first for the big money stuff, then Finesse to grind harder.

🔧 How to Install Addons

Get CurseForge, search for TradeSkillMaster and Auctionator, click install, you're done. These addons are basically cheating for gold-making — don't skip them.

🏦 Warband Bank & Cross-Character Gold

Use the Warband Bank to move gold and mats between toons. Farm on your main gatherer, send stuff to your crafter alt, pool all your gold into one character. This is how you scale.

🌞 Eversong Woods

Easiest zone, tons of herbs. Follow the rivers. This is where newbies should farm.

Route Strategy

Follow the rivers in a large loop. Herb and ore nodes spawn densely along waterways. Use Rich Soil to plant seeds for bonus herbs. This zone is ideal for dual Herb/Mining characters.

Hug the edges to avoid elevation hell. Wild Overload gives you stuff to kill for extra herbs. Good for rare herb types.

💨 Speed Set Guide

Go zoom zoom and make bank

⚡ Why You Need a Speed Set

Move at 240% speed and you'll clear Motherlode in 2 minutes instead of 5. Over 10 runs, that's 30 minutes saved. That's money.

Note: Building a speed set is a mid-to-long-term investment. If you're a brand new player with one character and limited gold, focus on gathering and basic gold-making first — come back to this once you have 50k+ gold and are ready to optimize your legacy farming.

🐱 Best Class: Feral Druid

Druids are broken for speed farming. Travel Form is instant mount, Cat Form is stealth. Just use Druid.

Runner-ups: Demon Hunter (double jump + Fel Rush), Monk (Roll), Hunter (Aspect of the Cheetah)

🏃 Best Races

Horde: Zandalari Trolls have +5% permanent speed. That's it. Pick this.

Alliance: Night Elves can stealth skip mobs. Pick this if Alliance.

🛡 Core Gear: Azerite Armor + Longstrider

The backbone of every speed set is BFA Azerite Armor with the Longstrider trait. This gives a permanent ~25% movement speed boost based on your highest secondary stat. You need three pieces (head, shoulders, chest) plus the Heart of Azeroth neck to activate the traits. Farm these on Normal mode only — normal versions give higher Longstrider values.

For every slot not covered by Azerite gear, buy cheap BoE greens from the AH that have the +Speed tertiary stat. For boots specifically, you need legacy boots that accept the Minor Speed enchant — The Sentinel's Eternal Refuge (~200–250k) is best-in-slot, or Shadebound Treads as a budget option.

Does It Pay For Itself?

A speed set costs 50–300k to assemble depending on boot choice, but pays for itself within a week of legacy farming. The Motherlode alone at 10 runs/hour with a speed set generates 10–17k GPH in pure vendor gold. Add in transmog drops from weekly raid clears and you're looking at 50k+ per week across alts — all while half-watching Netflix.

📈 Auction House Flipping

The highest ceiling gold method — for those with capital and patience

AH flipping is the art of buying items below market value and reselling at a profit. Unlike farming, it doesn't require killing mobs or leveling professions — but it demands market knowledge, starting capital, and the discipline to be patient. Top "goblins" make millions per week through flipping alone.

📊 Getting Started with Flipping

▼

Step 1: Build capital. You need at least 50k–100k gold to start flipping effectively. Use gathering or dungeon farms to build your starting fund.

Step 2: Install TSM. TradeSkillMaster is non-negotiable for serious AH work. Set up pricing operations, sniper scans, and shopping lists.

Step 3: Learn your markets. Spend a full week just watching prices. Note daily patterns, reset-day spikes, and which items have the widest price swings.

Step 4: Start small. Begin with high-volume commodities (herbs, ores, cloth) where you can buy in bulk at low points and sell into demand. The margins are thin but the volume is safe.

📋 Crafting Orders

Get paid to craft stuff

Max rank crafting = tips go HARD week 1. 50k-200k per order. By month 2 it drops to chump change. Grind KP on day 1 or lose thousands.

The system uses three order types — Public, Personal, and Guild — plus NPC Patron Orders for guaranteed weekly income. Your Artisan Moxie reputation grows as you fill orders, unlocking better Patron rewards over time.

Public Orders

Listed on the crafting order board for anyone to fill. Lower tips but higher volume. Great for leveling your skill and building Artisan Moxie. Public orders have a 30-minute completion timer once claimed — don't claim orders you can't fill quickly. If you fail to complete it, the order returns to the board and your Moxie takes a hit.

Personal Orders

Direct commissions from players who trust your skill level. Higher tips (often 5k–50k+ per craft). Build your reputation in trade chat and community discords. Personal orders also support Recrafting — upgrading existing gear quality.

Guild Orders

Fulfill orders for your guild members. Tips may be lower but volume is steady. Position yourself as the go-to crafter in your guild for reliable income. Guild orders are visible only to your guild's crafters.

Patron Orders

Weekly NPC crafting orders that provide free materials and guaranteed gold + Artisan Moxie. Complete these first every week — they're essentially free income. Higher Moxie unlocks better-paying Patron Orders over time. Check all your alts.

Hot Take

Advertise your max-rank specializations in Trade Chat with a clear format: profession, what you can craft at max rank, and your typical tip range. Players will add you to their friends list for repeat business. Consistency builds a customer base that pays for itself.

💹 Economy Tips

How the market actually works

📢 Patch Day Economy

New patch = market goes nuts. Stockpile before. First crafters make bank.

🏠 Server Population Matters

High-pop = more competition but more buyers. Low-pop = slower but less undercuts. Pick your poison.

🪙 WoW Token Timing

Tokens are cheap right now. If you buy game time with gold, buy now. If you sell gold for tokens, wait until everyone is rich.

The One Rule That Matters

Don't sell on reset day. Everyone is selling. Buy Saturday, sell Tuesday. That's the whole game.

📖 Glossary

The stuff you'll hear thrown around

🔑 Knowledge Points (KP)

The XP system for professions. You earn these weekly from quests, Treatises, and treasure finds. They're time-gated so you can't just no-life your way to max — everyone's on roughly the same pace.

⭐ Artisan Moxie

Your crafter reputation. Higher rep = better paying orders. Don't abandon orders or you'll lose it.

🧪 Concentration

Limited resource. Use it on expensive crafts. Don't blow it on trash.

📜 Treatises

Weekly KP items everyone needs. If you make these, you print money.

📋 Work Orders

How people commission you to make stuff. Public orders are quick, personal orders pay better. Don't abandon them or your rep tanks.

🔄 Recrafting

Upgrade someone else's gear to higher quality. Cheaper than making new, profitable for you.

💎 Unalloyed Abundance

Free ore procs when you smelt. Stack Mining spec to get more.

📊 GPH (Gold Per Hour)

Gold per hour. This is how you measure everything. Factor in AH cuts.
