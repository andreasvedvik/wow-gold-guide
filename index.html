⚔ Gold-Making Fundamentals

Core principles that separate the wealthy from the broke

📊 Diversify Income

Never rely on a single gold source. Markets crash, farms get nerfed, and patches shift demand. Spread your efforts across gathering, crafting, and flipping to stay resilient.

⏱ Gold Per Hour

Track your GPH (gold per hour) for every activity. Time spent flying between nodes or waiting on AH sales counts. Always ask: "Is this the best use of my time?"

📅 Timing Is Everything

Prices spike on Tuesday resets and when new content drops. Consumables surge on raid nights. Gather materials early in the week and sell into Tuesday/Wednesday demand.

🧠 Knowledge = Gold

Invest in learning your server's economy. Watch prices for a week before committing to a strategy. The Auction House rewards patience and punishes impulse.

🆕 New Player Basics

Essential knowledge before you start making gold — skip this if you're experienced

🏦 The Auction House (AH)

The AH is WoW's player-driven marketplace, located in every major city. Open it by finding an Auctioneer NPC (look for the gavel icon on your minimap). You can list items for sale with a deposit fee (you get it back if the item sells), and buy items other players have listed. Commodities (herbs, ores, cloth) sell on a region-wide AH, meaning you compete with every player in your region. Crafted gear sells on your server's local AH. The standard AH cut is 5% of the sale price.

📅 What's "Reset Day"?

Every Tuesday (NA) or Wednesday (EU), WoW resets weekly content: raid lockouts, weekly quests, Mythic+ vaults, and crafting patron orders. This triggers a surge in demand for consumables (flasks, potions, food, enchants, gems) as raiders prep for the week. Prices spike Tuesday–Wednesday and dip over the weekend when more casual players farm and flood supply. This weekly cycle is the single most important pattern for gold-making.

⚒ Profession Limits

Each character can learn exactly two primary professions (e.g., Herbalism + Mining, or Alchemy + Enchanting). You cannot have three. However, Fishing and Cooking are secondary professions — every character can learn them for free on top of your two primaries. Plan your profession pairs carefully: gathering + gathering (easy gold) or gathering + crafting (self-supply your crafter) are the most common combos for gold-making.

📊 Gathering Stats Explained

Perception increases your chance of finding rare materials (like Nocturnal Lotus or Brilliant Silver Ore). Finesse gives bonus yield per node — more herbs/ore per gather. Deftness speeds up your gathering cast time. These stats come from profession gear (crafted tools and accessories) and specialization nodes. Prioritize Perception early for rare procs, then Finesse for volume.

🔧 How to Install Addons

Addons are UI modifications that enhance your gameplay. To install them: download the CurseForge app (the most popular addon manager). Open it, select World of Warcraft, and search for addons like "TradeSkillMaster" or "Auctionator." Click Install and they'll be active next time you launch WoW. You can also use the WoWUp addon manager as an alternative. No manual file copying needed — the app handles everything.

🏦 Warband Bank & Cross-Character Gold

The Warband system lets you share gold, items, and resources across all your characters on the same account via the Warband Bank. This is crucial for gold-making: farm on your gatherer, send materials to your crafter, and funnel gold from alts into one pool. Access the Warband Bank tab in any regular bank NPC. If a guide says "do this on all alts," the Warband Bank is how you move materials and gold between them efficiently.

⛏ Gathering Professions

The most accessible and consistent gold sources in the game

Multiboxing's Impact on Gathering

Gathering is more profitable per-player now than in previous expansions. Blizzard's crackdown on input broadcasting software effectively ended multiboxing, which used to flood the market with cheap materials from 5-10 character farming teams. With that supply gone, solo gatherers earn significantly more per hour than the BFA/Shadowlands era.

All herbs spawn in every Midnight zone — pick your route based on mote type, not herb type. Harandar has the densest node spawns overall. Stack Perception for Nocturnal Lotus procs, which are worth significantly more per unit.

Mounted Gathering

Herbalism has a specialization node that lets you gather herbs while mounted — no dismounting needed. This is a massive GPH boost. Prioritize unlocking it early in your Botany specialization tree. Combined with a Druid's flight-form gathering, this makes herbalism the fastest gathering profession by far.

Quality Tiers

Gathered herbs in Midnight use a simplified 2-tier quality system (Tier 1 and Tier 2) instead of the 5-rank crafting system. Higher Finesse and Perception stats increase your chance of gathering Tier 2 materials, which sell for significantly more on the AH and are required for high-rank crafts.

⚠ Bot Activity Notice

Be aware that bot activity can crater gathering prices on some realms, especially during off-peak hours. If you notice herb prices consistently below crafting cost, bots may be flooding supply. Counter-strategy: sell into peak demand (Tuesday–Wednesday) when legitimate buyer volume absorbs the bot supply, or pivot to a less-botted material.

🔨 Crafting Professions

Higher effort, higher reward — the backbone of the WoW economy

Concentration — The Key to High-Rank Profits

Concentration is a limited resource that guarantees a higher quality result on any craft. It regenerates slowly over time across your account. The profit difference between a Rank 4 and Rank 5 craft can be 10x or more — use Concentration strategically on high-value crafts where the quality jump has the biggest price impact. Never waste it on low-margin items. This single mechanic is what separates profitable crafters from break-even ones.

🧪 Alchemy

The most consistently profitable crafting profession. Everyone needs flasks, potions, and cauldrons — demand spikes on raid nights and new content drops. Alchemy provides a reliable income stream that rarely dries up, plus housing dyes for the decor market.

  • Flasks and phials for raiders (constant demand)
  • Combat potions (used in M+ and raids)
  • Cauldrons for guild groups (high material cost, high profit margin)
  • Transmutations for daily cooldown profit
  • Housing dyes for recoloring decor items (new Midnight niche)
  • Treasure-finding potions (if available) — a goblin staple that increases gold/material drops from mobs. Check Wowhead for the Midnight equivalent at launch

Spec Trees: Alchemical Mastery, Potioncraft, Phial Mastery, Transmutation

Alchemy Procs & Mixology

Alchemists have a chance to proc extra items when crafting flasks and potions — this is a huge part of why Alchemy is Tier S. The Mixology passive also doubles your own flask duration, saving gold on personal consumables. Both effects scale with your specialization investment.

Tier S — Best consistent earner

The best dual-gathering zone in Midnight. Stick close to rivers where herb nodes cluster heavily. Look for Rich Soil patches along riverbanks to plant seeds for extra herbs. Smooth terrain makes this the most beginner-friendly zone with consistent node respawns.

💨 Speed Set Guide

Move faster, farm faster, earn faster — the secret to efficient legacy runs

⚡ Why You Need a Speed Set

A proper speed set can push your movement speed to 220–240%, turning a 5-minute dungeon clear into under 2 minutes. Over dozens of runs farming mounts or transmog, this saves hours. Legacy content doesn't challenge your combat power at level 80 — the only bottleneck is how fast you can move between packs and bosses.

Note: Building a speed set is a mid-to-long-term investment. If you're a brand new player with one character and limited gold, focus on gathering and basic gold-making first — come back to this once you have 50k+ gold and are ready to optimize your legacy farming.

🐱 Best Class: Feral Druid

Druid is the undisputed king of speed farming. Travel Form means instant mount with no cast time. Cat Form gives stealth to skip trash. Feline Swiftness adds a passive 15% speed bonus. Stack that with Dash (+60%), Stampeding Roar (+60%), and Tiger Dash for burst movement. No other class comes close.

Runner-ups: Demon Hunter (double jump + Fel Rush), Monk (Roll), Hunter (Aspect of the Cheetah)

🏃 Best Races

Horde: Zandalari Troll — Embrace of the Loa (Gonk) gives a permanent 5% movement speed buff. The best passive racial for speed farming, no contest.

Alliance: Night Elf — Shadowmeld lets you drop combat instantly to skip trash packs, and Wisp Spirit gives 75% faster ghost run speed if you die.

🛡 Core Gear: Azerite Armor + Longstrider

The backbone of every speed set is BFA Azerite Armor with the Longstrider trait. This gives a permanent ~25% movement speed boost based on your highest secondary stat. You need three pieces (head, shoulders, chest) plus the Heart of Azeroth neck to activate the traits. Farm these on Normal mode only — normal versions give higher Longstrider values.

📈 Auction House Flipping

The highest ceiling gold method — for those with capital and patience

AH flipping is the art of buying items below market value and reselling at a profit. Unlike farming, it doesn't require killing mobs or leveling professions — but it demands market knowledge, starting capital, and the discipline to be patient. Top "goblins" make millions per week through flipping alone.

📊 Getting Started with Flipping

▼

Step 1: Build capital. You need at least 50k–100k gold to start flipping effectively. Use gathering or dungeon farms to build your starting fund.

Step 2: Install TSM. TradeSkillMaster is non-negotiable for serious AH work. Set up pricing operations, sniper scans, and shopping lists.

Step 3: Learn your markets. Spend a full week just watching prices. Note daily patterns, reset-day spikes, and which items have the widest price swings.

Step 4: Start small. Begin with high-volume commodities (herbs, ores, cloth) where you can buy in bulk at low points and sell into demand. The margins are thin but the volume is safe.

Understanding Commodity Walls

A wall is when someone lists a massive quantity (50k-200k+) of a material at a fixed price. This anchors the market — nobody can sell above the wall price. When you see a wall, decide: is it underpriced (buy the wall for profit later) or overpriced (don't bother listing, wait for it to sell through). Learning to read walls is a core goblin skill.

🔁 Cancel Scanning — Staying on Top

▼

The AH is last-in-first-out for commodities — the cheapest listing sells first. If someone undercuts you by 1 copper, your listing goes to the back of the queue. Serious goblins run cancel scans every 15-30 minutes on raid nights to stay competitive.

TSM's Cancel Scan Feature: TSM can automate this process. It finds your undercut auctions, cancels them, and lets you repost at the new lowest price. This keeps your listings at the front of the queue so they sell before competitors.

Warning: Cancel scanning burns deposit gold on non-commodity items. Your deposit is only refunded when an item sells, so canceling and reposting costs you money on low-velocity items. Only use cancel scanning on high-margin items and commodities where the deposit is minimal and sales velocity is high.

⚠ Warning

The region-wide commodity AH means you're competing with every player in your region, not just your server. Margins on commodities are thinner than ever. Focus on learning price patterns and timing your purchases rather than trying to undercut constantly.

📋 Crafting Orders

Midnight's built-in commission system

Crafting Orders let players commission items from skilled crafters. If you've invested knowledge points to reach max-rank crafting in any profession, this is potentially the highest per-item gold method in the game. Building a reputation as a reliable crafter brings repeat customers. Tips follow a steep decline curve: Week 1 tips can reach 50k–200k+ per craft when few crafters have max rank. By weeks 2–4, tips drop to 10k–50k as more crafters catch up. By month 2, expect 2k–10k tips as the market saturates. Over 80% of your crafting order income for an entire season comes in the first 2–3 weeks. Invest in Knowledge Points aggressively at launch — every day you delay costs real gold.

The system uses three order types — Public, Personal, and Guild — plus NPC Patron Orders for guaranteed weekly income. Your Artisan Moxie reputation grows as you fill orders, unlocking better Patron rewards over time.

Public Orders

Listed on the crafting order board for anyone to fill. Lower tips but higher volume. Great for leveling your skill and building Artisan Moxie. Public orders have a 30-minute completion timer once claimed — don't claim orders you can't fill quickly. If you fail to complete it, the order returns to the board and your Moxie takes a hit.

Personal Orders

Direct commissions from players who trust your skill level. Higher tips (often 5k–50k+ per craft). Build your reputation in trade chat and community discords. Personal orders also support Recrafting — upgrading existing gear quality.

Guild Orders

Fulfill orders for your guild members. Tips may be lower but volume is steady. Position yourself as the go-to crafter in your guild for reliable income. Guild orders are visible only to your guild's crafters.

Patron Orders

Weekly NPC crafting orders that provide free materials and guaranteed gold + Artisan Moxie. Complete these first every week — they're essentially free income. Higher Moxie unlocks better-paying Patron Orders over time. Check all your alts.

Pro Tip

Advertise your max-rank specializations in Trade Chat with a clear format: profession, what you can craft at max rank, and your typical tip range. Players will add you to their friends list for repeat business. Consistency builds a customer base that pays for itself.

🔄 Material Shuffles

Convert cheap materials into expensive ones through profession chains

What Is a Shuffle?

A shuffle is a profession chain where you buy cheap Material A on the AH, process it through multiple professions to create expensive Material B, then sell the result for profit. The key is finding price inefficiencies — sometimes the raw materials are cheaper than the crafted output. Shuffles are discovered by the community in the first weeks of an expansion and spread through sites like r/woweconomy and the TSM Discord.

Early Discovery Phase

The exact Midnight shuffle will be discovered in the first 1-2 weeks. Check r/woweconomy and the TSM Discord for the latest chains. Early adopters who find shuffles before they go viral make the most gold.

Classic Shuffle Example

Ore → Gems → Disenchant Materials

Buy cheap ore → prospect into gems (Jewelcrafting) → cut low-value gems into rings/necklaces → disenchant into enchanting materials (Disenchanting) → sell shards/crystals or craft enchants with them. Each step adds value if priced correctly.

Vendor Shuffles

Some crafted items vendor for more than their material cost. This is rarer but happens with niche items. Check CraftSim's vendor profit column after every patch to discover new vendor shuffles. These often have shorter profit windows before the market corrects.

⚠ Shuffles Get Nerfed Fast

Once a shuffle goes viral on Reddit and Discord, everyone copies it. Supply floods the market, prices collapse, and the profit window closes. The best shuffles only last 1-3 weeks. Act fast once you identify a profitable chain, and keep your best shuffles private — don't advertise them publicly if you want to keep profiting.

💹 Economy Tips

Understanding the meta-game of WoW's economy

🌐 Region-Wide Commodity AH

All commodities (herbs, ores, cloth, consumables) now sell on a region-wide Auction House. This means more buyers but also more competition. Prices are more stable across realms, but margins are thinner. Timing your sales matters more than ever.

📆 The Weekly Cycle

Prices follow a predictable weekly pattern. Materials are cheapest on weekends (more farmers online). Consumables spike Tuesday–Wednesday (raid resets). Craft and list consumables Monday night. Buy materials on Saturday.

📢 Patch Day Economy

New patches and seasons cause massive economic shifts. New gear means demand for enchants, gems, and consumables explodes. Stockpile materials before a patch drops and sell into the surge. Early crafters of new recipes make the most gold.

🏠 Server Population Matters

High-pop servers have more buyers but fierce competition. Low-pop servers have slower sales but less undercutting. For crafted gear (non-commodity), your server's population still matters since these aren't region-wide.

🪙 WoW Token Timing

Tokens are cheapest at expansion launches — returning players buy Tokens with real money (increasing supply), while the in-game gold supply is still low (less demand from gold-rich players). Right now during Midnight's launch, Token prices are near historic lows. As the season progresses and players accumulate more gold, more people can afford Tokens, driving prices up. If you're buying Tokens with gold for game time, buy now while they're cheap. If you're selling Tokens for gold, wait until mid-season when gold-rich players push prices higher.

Golden Rule

Never sell materials on reset day — that's when everyone else is selling too. Buy on weekends when prices are lowest. Sell consumables and enchants Tuesday through Thursday when demand peaks. This simple timing adjustment alone can increase your profits by 20–30%.

📖 Glossary

Key terms every gold-maker should know

🔑 Knowledge Points (KP)

Profession progression currency earned weekly from quests, Treatises, treasures, and first-crafts. Spent in specialization trees to unlock bonuses and new recipes. Capped weekly — don't miss any sources.

⭐ Artisan Moxie

Reputation earned by completing crafting orders. Higher Moxie unlocks better-paying NPC Patron Orders and shows clients you're a reliable crafter. Lost if you abandon claimed orders.

🧪 Concentration

A limited resource that guarantees a higher quality result on a craft. Regenerates slowly over time. Use it strategically on high-value crafts where the quality jump significantly increases the sale price.

📜 Treatises

Weekly consumable items crafted by Inscriptionists that grant +1 Knowledge Point to a specific profession. Every crafter needs one per profession per week — massive demand makes these a reliable income source for Inscription.

📋 Work Orders

The crafting commission system. Three types: Public (open board, lower tips), Personal (direct, higher tips), and Guild (internal). Plus NPC Patron Orders for guaranteed weekly income. 30-minute timer on claimed public orders.

🔄 Recrafting

Upgrading existing crafted gear to a higher quality tier without making a new item from scratch. Done through Personal crafting orders. Costs fewer materials than a fresh craft — profitable service for skilled crafters.

💎 Unalloyed Abundance

A rare proc when smelting ore that yields bonus refined materials. Higher Mining specialization increases proc chance. Makes high-volume smelting more profitable over time.

📊 GPH (Gold Per Hour)

The standard metric for comparing farming methods. Always factor in AH fees (5%), deposit costs, and time spent listing. A farm that earns 50k GPH raw might net 45k after fees.
