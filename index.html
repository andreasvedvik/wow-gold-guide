‚öî Gold-Making Fundamentals

Core principles that separate the wealthy from the broke

üìä Diversify Income

Never rely on a single gold source. Markets crash, farms get nerfed, and patches shift demand. Spread your efforts across gathering, crafting, and flipping to stay resilient.

‚è± Gold Per Hour

Track your GPH (gold per hour) ‚Äî the standard way gold-makers measure efficiency. Time spent flying between nodes or waiting on AH sales counts. Always ask: "Is this the best use of my time?"

üìÖ Timing Is Everything

Prices spike on Tuesday resets (when raids and dungeons reset weekly, causing a surge in demand for consumables) and when new content drops. Consumables surge on raid nights. Gather materials early in the week and sell into Tuesday/Wednesday demand.

üß† Knowledge = Gold

Invest in learning your server's economy. Watch prices for a week before committing to a strategy. The Auction House rewards patience and punishes impulse.

üå± New Player Guide

Your roadmap for the first week of gold-making in Midnight

üìÖ Day 1: Pick Your Professions

Visit a profession trainer in Silvermoon City (the main hub in Midnight). You can learn two primary professions per character. For your first character, we recommend Herbalism + Mining (dual gathering) ‚Äî zero investment, immediate income. You'll find trainers near the crafting areas in Silvermoon.

üó∫ Day 2-3: Learn Your Routes

Install GatherMate2 and Routes addons (see Essential Addons below). Fly loops around Eversong Woods collecting every herb and ore node you see. The addons will map nodes automatically and create efficient routes after a few laps.

üí∞ Day 4-5: Sell Everything Raw

Don't craft anything yet. List all herbs and ores on the Auction House (AH). Use Auctionator addon for a beginner-friendly selling interface ‚Äî it shows you the current market price and lets you list with one click. Collect your gold from the mailbox.

üéØ Day 6-7: Evaluate & Expand

Check how much gold you made this week. Use that as your baseline GPH. Now you can decide: stick with gathering, try a crafting profession, or start saving for AH flipping capital. Read the Quick Start Tier List below to pick your next move.

üìñ WoW Jargon Glossary

Essential Terms

  • AH ‚Äî Auction House, where players buy and sell items
  • GPH ‚Äî Gold Per Hour, the standard metric for measuring farm efficiency
  • BoE ‚Äî Bind on Equip, items that can be traded until worn
  • BoP ‚Äî Bind on Pickup, items that cannot be traded
  • M+ ‚Äî Mythic Plus, repeatable endgame dungeons with scaling difficulty
  • TSM ‚Äî TradeSkillMaster, the most powerful gold-making addon
  • Goblin ‚Äî A player focused on making gold (named after the WoW race known for commerce)
  • Flipping ‚Äî Buying items cheap and reselling at a higher price
  • Transmog ‚Äî Cosmetic appearances collected from gear, sold to collectors
  • Knowledge Points ‚Äî Profession skill points earned through quests and discoveries that unlock specialization talents, making your crafts higher quality
  • Concentration ‚Äî A resource pool (up to ~1000 points, regenerates ~100/day) you spend to guarantee higher quality crafts
  • Follower Dungeons ‚Äî A system that lets you run dungeons solo with NPC companions instead of other players
  • Reset Day ‚Äî Tuesday (NA) / Wednesday (EU) when raids and dungeons reset, causing demand spikes

üìà Auction House Flipping

The highest ceiling gold method ‚Äî for those with capital and patience

AH flipping is the art of buying items below market value and reselling at a profit. Unlike farming, it doesn't require killing mobs or leveling professions ‚Äî but it demands market knowledge, starting capital, and the discipline to be patient. Top "goblins" make millions per week through flipping alone.

üìä Getting Started with Flipping

‚ñº

Step 1: Build capital. You need at least 50k‚Äì100k gold to start flipping effectively. Use Midnight gathering or dungeon farms to build your starting fund.

Step 2: Install TSM. TradeSkillMaster is non-negotiable for serious AH work. Set up pricing operations, sniper scans, and shopping lists for region-wide commodity tracking.

Step 3: Learn your markets. Spend a full week just watching prices. Note daily patterns, reset-day spikes, and which items have the widest price swings in Midnight's economy.

Step 4: Start small. Begin with high-volume commodities (herbs, ores, cloth) where you can buy in bulk at low points and sell into demand. The margins are thin but the volume is safe with region-wide markets.

üìã Crafting Orders

Midnight's commission system for skilled crafters

Crafting Orders in Midnight let players commission items from skilled crafters. If you've invested knowledge points to reach max-rank crafting in any profession, this is potentially the highest per-item gold method in the game. Building a reputation as a reliable crafter brings repeat customers. Use Silvermoon's two crafting zones to manage multiple professions efficiently.

Public Orders

Listed on the crafting order board for anyone to fill. Lower tips but higher volume. Great for leveling your skill and building patron reputation.

Personal Orders

Direct commissions from players who trust your skill level. Higher tips (often 5k‚Äì50k+ per craft). Build your reputation in trade chat and community discords.

Guild Orders

Fulfill orders for your guild members. Tips may be lower but volume is steady. Position yourself as the go-to crafter in your guild for reliable income.

Patron Orders

Weekly NPC crafting orders that provide free materials and guaranteed income. Complete these first every week ‚Äî they're essentially free gold. Check all your alts.

Pro Tip

Advertise your max-rank specializations in Trade Chat with a clear format: profession, what you can craft at max rank, and your typical tip range. Players will add you to their friends list for repeat business. Consistency builds a customer base that pays for itself.

‚è∞ Timing Your Crafting Orders

When you complete crafting orders matters as much as what you craft. The best gold from public orders comes mid-to-late week:

  • Mid-week (Wed-Thu): Many crafters have exhausted their weekly public order quota. Less competition = bigger tips on remaining orders.
  • Before raid hours: Players rush to get gear crafted before raid night. They'll pay premium tips for fast turnaround.
  • After raid hours: Raiders want upgrades after getting new crests. Quick crafters catch the post-raid rush.
  • Save Concentration: Concentration is a resource pool (not a single charge) that regenerates slowly over time. Different crafts cost different amounts. Spend it on your highest-value craft that needs the quality boost most ‚Äî never waste Concentration on items you can already naturally hit Silver on.

üè† Player Housing Economy

Midnight's newest gold frontier ‚Äî decorating is big business

Player Housing is one of Midnight's headline features, and every profession can craft placeable decor items. Housing decor recipes span all expansion tiers (Classic through Midnight), meaning old profession skills suddenly have new value. This is a brand-new market with volatile prices and huge opportunity.

ü™µ Lumber

All housing decor requires Lumber alongside profession materials. Lumber gathering doesn't require a profession ‚Äî you get an axe from your neighborhood NPC. Farm lumber while doing other activities for free crafting materials.

üé® Dyes (Alchemy)

Alchemy can create dyes used to recolor housing items. This is a unique niche that adds another revenue stream on top of the usual consumable market.

üìú Decor Crafting

Inscription, Blacksmithing, Tailoring, Engineering, Jewelcrafting, and Leatherworking all have housing decor recipes. Check your recipe list ‚Äî some of the most expensive decor items come from obscure old-world recipe tiers.

üí∞ Market Opportunity

Housing decor is a brand-new market with no established price history. Early crafters setting prices have an advantage. Watch for which decor items trend on social media ‚Äî popular designs sell at premium prices. Start with cheap-to-craft items (wooden furniture from Lumber + basic mats) to learn the market, then invest in expensive rare recipes.

Goblin Insight

Don't sleep on old-world profession tiers. Housing decor recipes pull from Classic, TBC, Wrath, and other expansion skill trees. If you have a neglected alt with maxed Pandaria Enchanting or Wrath Blacksmithing, they might suddenly be sitting on a gold mine of exclusive decor recipes.

üíπ Economy Tips

Understanding the meta-game of WoW's economy

üåê Region-Wide Commodity AH

All commodities (herbs, ores, cloth, consumables) now sell on a region-wide Auction House. This means more buyers but also more competition. Prices are more stable across realms, but margins are thinner. Timing your sales matters more than ever.

üìÜ The Weekly Cycle

Prices follow a predictable weekly pattern. Materials are cheapest on weekends (more farmers online). Consumables spike Tuesday‚ÄìWednesday (raid resets). Craft and list consumables Monday night. Buy materials on Saturday.

üì¢ Patch Day Economy

New patches and seasons cause massive economic shifts. New gear means demand for enchants, gems, and consumables explodes. Stockpile materials before a patch drops and sell into the surge. Early crafters of new recipes make the most gold.

üè† Server Population Matters

High-pop servers have more buyers but fierce competition. Low-pop servers have slower sales but less undercutting. For crafted gear (non-commodity), your server's population still matters since these aren't region-wide.

Golden Rule

Never sell materials on reset day ‚Äî that's when everyone else is selling too. Buy on weekends when prices are lowest. Sell consumables and enchants Tuesday through Thursday when demand peaks. On Midnight launch, expect token prices to spike 30-50% at expansion release. This simple timing adjustment alone can increase your profits by 20‚Äì30%.

ü™ô WoW Token Timing

If your goal is buying game time with gold, when you buy the token matters as much as how much gold you make. Token prices follow predictable patterns:

  • Cheapest: Mid-week (Tuesday-Thursday), mid-month, during content droughts
  • Most expensive: Expansion launches, major patch drops, holiday events, beginning of month
  • Season resets & patches: Token prices spike 30-50% when new seasons and major patches drop. Buy tokens during content droughts when prices are lowest
  • Track prices at wowtoken.info ‚Äî set alerts for price drops below your threshold
Pro Tip

If you're earning 200k+/week passively (see Alt Army Economics), you can fund a permanent WoW subscription with gold alone. That's the real endgame for most goblins.

üìä Price-Checking Methodology

Knowing which crafts or flips are profitable right now requires reading data, not guessing. Here's how experienced goblins evaluate every trade:

  • TSM Tooltips: Hover any item ‚Äî check "DBMarket" (your server) vs "DBRegionMarketAvg" (all servers). If your server price is 20%+ above region, sell. If below, buy.
  • Crafting Cost: TSM shows "Crafting Cost" vs "DBMarket" on crafted items. Only craft when Market > Cost √ó 1.15 (to cover AH cut + risk).
  • Undermine Exchange (undermine.exchange) ‚Äî check price history graphs. Avoid items on a downward trend. Buy into items with cyclical patterns at their trough.
  • Region vs Server: If an item sells for 100g on your server but 500g region-wide, someone is dumping. Buy their stock and wait for the price to normalize.
  • Sale Rate: TSM's "RegionSaleRate" tells you how fast an item moves. Anything above 0.2 is fast. Below 0.05 is transmog-tier patience.
‚≠ê Crafting Quality Tiers

Every crafted item in Midnight has quality ranks from Rank 1 (lowest) to Rank 5 (highest). Higher ranks give better stats and sell for dramatically more gold. Your crafting skill, specialization talents, and Concentration spending all determine what rank you can hit.

  • Rank 1-2: Low quality ‚Äî barely sells above material cost. Avoid crafting at these ranks for profit.
  • Rank 3 (Silver): The baseline for sellable gear. Most crafters can hit this with moderate Knowledge Point investment.
  • Rank 4-5 (Gold/Orange): Premium quality ‚Äî commands massive premiums and tip amounts. Requires deep specialization + Concentration. This is where the real gold is.

The quality system is why Knowledge Points matter so much ‚Äî each point invested pushes your crafting skill higher, letting you hit better quality tiers consistently.

üîÆ Understanding Concentration

Concentration is Midnight's system for guaranteeing higher-quality crafts. It's a resource pool (up to 1000 points) that regenerates slowly over time ‚Äî roughly 100 points per day. When spent on a craft, it significantly boosts your chance of reaching a higher quality tier.

  • It's a pool, not a charge ‚Äî different crafts cost different amounts of Concentration (some high-end items cost 200+)
  • Spend it on your highest-value craft ‚Äî the item furthest from guaranteed Gold quality without help
  • Never waste it on cheap items you can already hit Silver quality on naturally
  • Gear crafter alts each have their own Concentration pool ‚Äî more alts = more high-quality crafts per day
üìà Expansion Economy Phases

Midnight's economy revolves around three distinct phases. Knowing which phase you're in determines what to farm and sell:

  • Launch Phase (Week 1-3): Raw materials and consumables sell instantly at inflated prices. Gathering professions dominate. Everyone is leveling professions and there's zero existing supply.
  • Stabilization Phase (Week 4-8): Crafted gear and enchants take over as the primary gold source. This is when Concentration is most valuable ‚Äî fewer crafters have reached max-rank recipes yet, so those who have can charge premium tips. Material prices normalize but crafting margins peak.
  • Late Season: Margins thin on consumables. Transmog, mounts, and niche items hold value. Smart goblins stockpile materials for the next patch/season reset.
Strategy

Build a diversified portfolio: one liquid gathering route, one endgame consumable category, and one crafting/service lane. Rotate focus based on which economy phase you're in.
