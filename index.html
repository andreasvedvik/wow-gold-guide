⚔ Gold-Making Fundamentals

The stuff that actually matters — ignore this and you'll stay broke

📊 Don't Put All Your Gold in One Bag

If you're only doing one thing to make gold, you're one hotfix away from being broke. Markets crash, farms get nerfed, and that herb that was 50g yesterday is 5g today. Spread your gold-making across a few different methods so when Blizz inevitably nerfs your favorite farm, you've got backup.

⏱ Gold Per Hour

Seriously, track your GPH. If you're flying loops for 2 hours and earning less than you could from other methods, switch strategies. Every minute spent farming is a minute not spent on something more profitable. Time is your most finite resource — don't waste it on low-GPH activities.

📅 Timing Is Everything

Tuesday reset is the highest-demand day for consumables and gear. Raid groups panic-buy flasks, potions, enchants, and repairs. Prices spike hard on Tuesday-Wednesday. Gather early in the week when supply is abundant and prices are low, then sell everything into the Tuesday demand spike. This timing pattern repeats every single week.

🧠 Knowledge = Gold

This one separates the rich from the broke. Spend a week just watching your server. Where do prices go? When do they tank? The AH rewards patients and punishes panic. Don't be impulsive.

⚙ Midnight Profession System Changes

Midnight makes several streamlining changes to the profession systems that affect all gold makers. Understanding these changes is crucial for efficient profit:

  • 2 Quality Levels for Consumables: Down from 3 tiers. This simplifies crafting and reduces the complexity of quality target calculation while maintaining profit differentiation.
  • Artisan's Moxie (New): Replaces Artisan's Acuity. This new currency is profession-specific — you can't share Alchemy Moxie with Enchanting. Plan knowledge builds around Moxie acquisition for each profession.
  • Crafting Orders at Midnight Skill 15: Unlocked at just skill level 15 in any Midnight profession (not overall skill — just 15 points in your Midnight expansion track). This means you can start taking crafting orders within minutes of picking up a new profession and earning commission gold on your first hour of leveling.
  • Patron Orders Contain Ingenious Identifier: New currency item that drops from patron crafting orders, useful for niche crafting requirements.
  • Reagent Bags in Dedicated Slot: New dedicated slot for reagent bags separate from regular bags, allowing you to carry more actual materials without bloating inventory.
  • Large Stack Sizes: Materials stack to 1000, consumables stack to 200. This means you need far fewer bag slots to store bulk materials compared to previous expansions.

🆕 New Player Basics

Foundation concepts that power all gold-making strategies

🚀 Your First Gold-Making Session — Do This Now

This is your entire roadmap for the first hour. Follow these 5 steps and you'll have your first gold pile by tomorrow:

  1. Pick Herbalism + Mining at the trainer in Silvermoon City (or your starting capital). No investment needed.
  2. Buy green profession equipment from the AH. Should cost under 500g total for both professions.
  3. Fly a loop around southern Eversong Woods gathering everything you see. Spend 30-60 minutes collecting herbs and ore.
  4. Return to the AH and list everything using "Post at Market Price" for simplicity. Don't overthink pricing — let the AH set it.
  5. Check the AH next day — your gold has arrived. Repeat daily, selling everything on Tuesdays for peak prices.

Expected results: Week 1 casual farming = 10k-30k gold. This method is boring but reliable. It's your foundation for everything else.

🏦 The Auction House (AH)

The AH is the central marketplace for trading items. Find an Auctioneer NPC (gavel icon on your map) to list items for sale or browse listings. When your item sells, Blizzard takes a 5% cut and you pocket the rest. Commodities (herbs, ore, cloth) trade on a region-wide AH with massive volume and competition. Crafted gear is server-specific, so competition is localized. Understanding which market you're competing in is essential for pricing.

📅 What's "Reset Day"?

Reset day is Tuesday when mythic+ and raid instances reset, and guilds prepare for raid night. Suddenly there's massive demand for flasks, potions, enchants, repairs, and stat food. Prices spike hard on Tuesday and stay elevated through Wednesday. By the weekend, casual players have farmed everything to death and supply crashes prices. Understanding this weekly cycle is more valuable than any other single pattern — it applies to basically every profitable item.

⚒ Profession Slots & Pairing

Each character gets two primary profession slots and unlimited secondary slots. Primary professions: choose two from Herbalism, Mining, Skinning, Alchemy, Enchanting, Blacksmithing, Jewelcrafting, Tailoring, Inscription, Leatherworking, Engineering. Secondary professions (Fishing, Cooking, Alchemy counts as both) can be done on every character without taking slots. Smart pairing: Herbalism + Alchemy (gather herbs, craft potions), Mining + Jewelcrafting (gather ore, cut gems), or pair any gathering with a crafting profession.

📊 Gathering Stats Explained

Perception increases your chance of rare procs like Nocturnal Lotus — stack this early for maximum profit on rare drops. Finesse increases the quantity of materials you gather per node and chance of higher-quality gathers — stack this for volume farming. Deftness speeds up gather time slightly. Priority: Perception first (big profit multiplier), then Finesse for grinding efficiency.

🔧 How to Install Addons

Download CurseForge desktop app, search for addon names (TradeSkillMaster, CraftSim, Routes, GatherMate2, Auctionator), click install. WoW automatically loads them on login. These aren't cheating — they're information tools that level the playing field. Addon-less gold-making is viable but handicaps you significantly compared to experienced players. Install the essentials before you start farming.

🛠 Profession Equipment Tiers

Profession gear dramatically affects crafting speed and quality. Understanding the tiers helps you plan your equipment progression:

  • Green (Uncommon) Equipment: Tradeable on the AH. Buy cheaply and upgrade as you level. Easy to acquire but minimal stats.
  • Blue (Rare) Equipment: Bind on Pickup from Crafting Orders only. Can only obtain through work orders on these items. Superior stats compared to green gear.
  • Epic Equipment: Bind on Pickup from Crafting Orders only. Highest-tier profession gear with excellent stat bonuses. Difficult to obtain — requires high-rank crafters and premium materials.
  • Strategy: Start with green AH gear while leveling. As you unlock better recipes and can take higher-tier crafting orders, invest in blue/epic gear for the stat benefits. The stat bonuses stack across all your professions, so prioritize your most profitable professions first.
🏦 Warband Bank & Cross-Character Gold Pooling

The Warband Bank is shared storage accessible from all your characters. Use it to move materials and gold between toons. Strategy: gather on your main, send materials to a dedicated crafter alt, then consolidate all gold into your banking alt. This lets you scale up farming (main stays on gathering instead of selling), and you accumulate capital for bigger flipping operations. Without Warband Bank, each character's economy is isolated — with it, you're managing an account-wide operation.

💰 How Much Gold Is Normal?

Don't compare yourself to endgame goblins — here are realistic benchmarks for new players:

  • First week (casual): 10k-30k (from questing + vendoring + selling a few herbs)
  • First week (focused farming): 50k-150k
  • First month (casual): 100k-300k
  • First month (dedicated farming): 500k-1M+

Gold cap: 10,000,000 per character (Warband shared) — you won't hit this for months.

For reference: WoW Token costs ~200-300k gold (varies by week). If you make 50k in week 1, that's 1/5 of a token — solid progress.

Golden rule: Don't compare yourself to r/woweconomy goblins — they play the AH like a full-time job and have months of capital stacked. You're starting from zero, and that's fine.

📈 AH Flipping

AH flipping is buying underpriced items and relisting at market value — the most scalable gold method in the game, but it requires capital, patience, and market knowledge. Start with consumable flipping: scan for flasks, potions, and enchants listed 15–20% below regional average (use Auctionator or TSM to automate this). Buy Sunday night, relist Tuesday afternoon. Material flipping works the same way — herbs and ore crash on weekends when farmers dump stock, then rebound midweek when crafters buy. The advanced play is reset flipping: buy out an entire item's supply when it dips below crafting cost, then relist everything at a controlled price. This is high-risk but can yield 100k+ in a single move. Key rules: never invest more than 20% of your liquid gold in one flip, always check the item's sale rate (not just price) on Undermine Exchange, and accept that some flips will lose money — your win rate just needs to be above 60%.

🌿 Herbalism

▼

Herbalism is one of the most reliable earners in every expansion. Raiding guilds burn through hundreds of flasks and potions weekly, which means herbs are always in demand. In Midnight, all six herbs spawn in every zone, so your route choice comes down to which mote type you want.

What Are Knowledge Points?

Knowledge Points (KP) are a separate progression system for each profession — they're NOT the same as skill levels. You spend KP in specialization trees to unlock abilities, improve quality, and unlock recipes. KP are TIME-GATED — you earn a limited amount per week, meaning early Knowledge Point investment decisions are CRITICAL. You can't easily respec, so choose wisely. The player who invests KP wisely reaches max quality first and makes the most gold while others are still grinding.

Herb Modifiers — Understanding Node Types

In Midnight, many herb nodes spawn with elemental modifiers that add special mechanics and mote drops. Understanding these modifiers is essential for maximizing your gold per hour:

  • Lightfused Nodes: Spawn light orbs around the node when gathered. Catch them midair for Motes of Light. Overloading a Lightfused node spawns a large burst of light orbs — rush to catch them before they hit the ground.
  • Wild Nodes: Spawn two hostile earth elementals that can be defeated and looted for additional resources. Overloading a Wild node summons an Elite Bear (Avatar of Halazzi variant) — defeat it to gain the Wild Perception buff for 5 minutes, granting +150 Perception for more rare drops.
  • Primal Nodes: Apply a debuff that slows you and deals minor damage over time. Druids can remove this by shapeshifting. Overloading spawns green orbs on the ground that grant bonus Motes of Primal Energy when you run through them.
  • Voidbound Nodes: A void portal appears and pulls you in (no damage dealt). The portal mechanic is key here. DON'T overload Voidbound nodes until you've unlocked the Voidbound sub-specialization, which grants a portal back — otherwise you can end up teleported deep into caves or other zones with no way back.
  • Lush Nodes: Found wherever base herbs grow and give extra resources beyond normal node yields.
  • Transplanted Nodes: Only found in Voidstorm, these are weaker versions of nodes from other zones.
Herb Modifiers — Difficulty Progression

Note for new players: Don't worry about node modifiers until you're around skill 50+. While leveling, just gather everything you see — the modifiers will make more sense once you've unlocked the Overload ability and have some KP invested.

Sky Golem Nerf

Critical Change: Sky Golem mounts no longer allow herbing while mounted in Midnight. This is a HUGE nerf to herbalists who relied on this mechanic in previous expansions. You must now unlock the mounted gathering specialization node under Botany to gather while mounted. Prioritize this unlock immediately as it's essential for competitive GPH.

Knowledge Build Recommendation

Method's Recommended Path: Unlock Midnight Overload tree first to reduce Overload cooldown (massively increases efficiency). Then invest in Botany → Mulching → Imbued Mulch, which converts 10 Tranquility Bloom into a guaranteed Nocturnal Lotus. This is crucial since Nocturnal Lotus is the highest-value herb.

Alternative (Wowhead): Start with Botany (40 points unlocks mounted gathering), then Bountiful Harvests for increased overall yield per node.

Herbalism Teas Buff Your Farming

New in Midnight: Cooking professions craft special teas that buff gatherers. Use these during your farming sessions:

  • Azeroot Tea: Grants Deftness buff (faster gathering speed)
  • Sanguithorn Tea: Grants Perception buff (more rare drops)
  • Argentleaf Tea: Grants Finesse buff (more total resources per node)
Stat Preference Debate — Community Consensus

Early Access Data: The community consensus from first-week farming reveals a clear hierarchy:

  • Deftness (Majority Pick): Faster gathering speed + synergy with mounted gathering = pick faster and escape before aggro. Many players dedicate to full Deftness specs.
  • Finesse (Solid Choice): "9–14 tier 2 materials per herb hits different" — more total resources per node adds up quickly. Strong for volume farming.
  • Perception (Zone-Dependent): Most players say "absolutely not worth it" as a primary stat. EXCEPTION: Nocturnal Lotus and Dazzling Thorium farming specifically benefit from Perception — rare procs = huge per-unit value. Consider Perception for specialty runs, not general farming.
  • Pro Tip: Use Azeroot Tea to get Deftness buff from consumable, then stack Finesse on gear. This hybrid approach maximizes both speed and per-node yield.

Route Strategy

All herbs spawn in every Midnight zone, so pick your route based on mote type rather than herb type. Harandar has the densest node spawns overall. Stack Perception on your profession gear for more Nocturnal Lotus procs — these are worth significantly more per unit than common herbs.

Mounted Gathering

Herbalism has a specialization node that lets you gather while mounted — no dismounting required. This is a massive GPH increase. Prioritize unlocking it early in your Botany specialization tree. Druids with flight-form gathering have a natural advantage here, making them the best herbalism class by far.

Quality Tiers

Gathered herbs use a simplified 2-tier quality system (Tier 1 and Tier 2). Higher Finesse and Perception stats increase your chance of gathering Tier 2 materials, which sell for significantly more on the AH and are required for high-rank crafts. Prioritize Perception early for rare procs, then Finesse for volume.

⚠ Bot Activity

Some servers experience heavy bot activity that craters herb prices. If your server's herb prices are unusually low, consider farming during off-peak hours, selling into Tuesday reset demand when prices spike regardless, or switching to a different gold method until prices recover.

The Lure System — MANDATORY for All Materials

Midnight introduces a completely new lure system that's ESSENTIAL for skinners. Unlike previous expansions, you must craft and use lures to summon Renowned Beasts — killing and skinning these beasts is the ONLY way to get Majestic materials, which are the highest-value skinning drops. The lure system works as follows:

  • Craftable Lures: 5 lures per zone + Grand variants that summon Renowned Beasts
  • Lure Materials: Fish are required as materials — this creates a link between Fishing and Skinning professions
  • Daily Cooldown (Not Hourly): Community Data: Players report that lures appear to be on a daily cooldown, not hourly — "I was not able to place a second one on any of the places." Only the first Renowned Beast summoned per day drops Majestic materials.
  • Majestic Drops Are EXTREMELY Rare: Early Access Report: One player with 40 Knowledge Points in Talented Tracker obtained only 2 fins and 1 hide over 2 days of lure farming. This is extreme scarcity. Majestic materials are expensive on the AH and will remain so.
  • Three Majestic Materials: Majestic Fin, Majestic Claw, Majestic Hide — used in high-tier crafted gear
  • Cost Per Attempt: Lure materials (fish) cost 2–8k gold depending on AH prices. Failed lure runs cost real gold, so budget accordingly.

High Value Beasts & Minimap Tracking

Midnight introduces a new minimap tracking system for Renowned Beasts. These high-value beasts drop approximately 3-4× more materials per skin than regular mobs. Use this system to locate and farm specific beast types that match your zone choice.

Diffusers — Infinite Mote Farming

Craft Diffusers using 2 motes, then use them on beasts before killing. Diffusers yield approximately double the motes back — and they have NO cooldown. This means you can farm infinite motes once you have initial capital. Set up a rotation: kill beasts with Diffusers → collect motes → craft more Diffusers → repeat.

The Majestic Materials sub-spec is CRITICAL for accessing high-tier skinning income.

Concentration is a limited resource that guarantees a higher quality result on any craft. It regenerates slowly over time across your account. ✨ Enchant Priority List — What Raiders Buy First

Enchants are mandatory, and raiders prioritize them in a specific order. Understanding this hierarchy helps you predict demand spikes:

  • Weapon Enchant (MANDATORY): The biggest stat boost. Every raider enchants their weapon first. Most expensive per-item cost.
  • Ring Enchants x2 (MANDATORY): Every raider needs both rings enchanted. 2 per character = massive volume.
  • Chest Enchant (High Priority): Large stat boost, applies to main armor piece.
  • Bracer Enchant (Medium Priority): Good stats, purchased after the essentials are covered.
  • Cloak Enchant (Medium Priority): Optional for many builds but some specializations need it.
  • Boot Enchant (Lower Priority): Stat vs. speed tradeoff. Purchased last, many raiders skip this.

Sales strategy: Stock weapon and ring enchants first — these have guaranteed demand. Chest enchants follow. Don't waste crafting resources on boot enchants in week 1; focus on high-priority items where demand is unlimited.

⚡ Crafted Gear BiS Breakpoints — Timing Your Sales

The Runed Crest system fills 2 gear slots with a spark, making crafted gear temporarily BiS (Best in Slot) during the early weeks:

  • Week 1-2: Crafted gear is BiS in most slots (Heroic raid equivalent). Prices peak. Demand is highest because raiders want immediate upgrades.
  • Week 3-4: Mythic raid drops start replacing crafted pieces. Prices crater as raiders no longer need them.
  • Rule of thumb: Sell crafted gear HARD in weeks 1-3. Don't hold inventory past week 3 expecting prices to recover.
  • Rank 5 premium: Rank 5 crafted gear commands premium tips in crafting orders because it minimizes the power loss compared to raid drops. Prioritize investing Knowledge Points to reach Rank 5 early.

Pro strategy: Identify which gear slots have the longest "BiS window" (e.g., trinkets stay valuable longer than armor). Prioritize crafting those slots first.

📈 Auction House Flipping

The highest ceiling gold method — for those with capital and patience

AH flipping is the art of buying items below market value and reselling at a profit. Unlike farming, it doesn't require killing mobs or leveling professions — but it demands market knowledge, starting capital, and the discipline to be patient. Top "goblins" make millions per week through flipping alone.

📊 Getting Started with Flipping

▼

Step 1: Build capital. You need at least 50k–100k gold to start flipping effectively. Use gathering or dungeon farms to build your starting fund.

Step 2: Install TSM. TradeSkillMaster is non-negotiable for serious AH work. Set up pricing operations, sniper scans, and shopping lists.

Step 3: Learn your markets. Spend a full week just watching prices. Note daily patterns, reset-day spikes, and which items have the widest price swings.

Step 4: Start small. Begin with high-volume commodities (herbs, ores, cloth) where you can buy in bulk at low points and sell into demand. The margins are thin but the volume is safe.

Understanding Market Walls

A "wall" is a massive stack of listings at a single price point, often placed intentionally by a major player to control the market. If a wall appears at a price you know is too high, either buy through it to break it (forcing the seller to relist lower) or wait for natural demand to sell through it. Walls at genuinely underpriced levels should be bought aggressively — they're profit opportunities.

Worked Example: Reading a Commodity Wall

Scenario: You open the AH and see 5,000 Tranquility Bloom at 22g, 200 at 18g, and 50 at 12g.

  • Analysis: The 12g and 18g listings are panic sellers or bait — ignore them
  • Real market: 22g is where the wall sits — that's your actual market price
  • Strategy: Buy everything under 20g (250 herbs × avg 16g = 4,000g investment)
  • Execute: Relist at 21g99s → sell into the wall → profit ~1,500g minus AH cut
  • Risk: If the wall breaks (someone dumps 10k herbs at 15g), you're underwater

Key insight: Walls are opportunities only if you understand the difference between panic-seller noise and actual market price.

💹 Economy Tips

Principles that explain market behavior and how to capitalize on them

🌐 Region-Wide Commodity AH

Commodities (herbs, ore, cloth, etc.) are traded on a region-wide AH, not server-specific. This means massive volume and competition but also stable prices. You can't corner the market on commodities because supply is nearly infinite. Instead, focus on volume — small margins multiplied by massive sales.

📆 The Weekly Cycle

Predictable pricing patterns emerge: farmers kill mobs and gather most on weekends, flooding the market with supply Saturday-Sunday. Prices hit bottom. Prices climb through Monday-Tuesday as raiders panic-buy for raids. Reset day (Tuesday) triggers volume spike. Buy during the supply glut (Saturday), sell into demand (Tuesday). This pattern repeats every week like clockwork.

📢 Patch Day Economy

New patches create market chaos because information is scarce and demand patterns shift. Stockpile materials before a patch drops — the first wave of crafters with max recipes and materials locked in make enormous profits before competition floods in. By day 3-4, margins normalize. By week 2, the meta settles and easy money is gone.

🏠 Server Population Matters

High-population realms have more competition (more farmers, flippers, crafters) but also more buyers and volume. Low-population realms have less competition and faster list turnover, but fewer buyers means items take longer to sell. Choose your server based on your playstyle: high-pop for AH flipping, low-pop for crafting orders.

🪙 WoW Token Timing

Token prices fluctuate based on gold supply in the economy. When gold is abundant (economy crash, dupes, RMT), tokens are cheap (good time to buy game time with gold). When gold is scarce (early expansion), tokens are expensive (good time to sell gold for tokens). Track token price trends — they indicate economy health.

📊 When to Hold vs When to Dump

Not all items should be held — know when to sell immediately and when to stockpile:

  • HOLD: Consumables (flasks, potions, food, runes) → demand spikes at Season 1 raid/M+ opening. Supply is finite.
  • HOLD: Rare crafting materials with daily cooldowns (Wondrous Synergist) → supply can't flood market
  • DUMP NOW: Common gathering materials (herbs, ore) → supply only increases as more players join
  • DUMP NOW: Rapidly depreciating items (Majestic materials, early-expansion exclusives) → price is cratering daily
  • SELL STEADY: Enchants, gems, consumables → always in demand, post daily rather than hoarding

Golden rule: "If you can't explain WHY the price will go up, sell now." Holding requires a reason.

📖 Glossary

WoW economy and crafting terminology explained

🔑 Knowledge Points (KP)

The progression currency for profession specializations. You earn KP weekly from quests, Treatises (purchased or crafted), Patron Orders, and world treasures. The system is heavily time-gated — each profession can gain only a fixed amount of KP per week, so everyone advances at roughly the same pace regardless of playtime. This prevents paying more gold from outright skipping progression.

⭐ Artisan Moxie

Your reputation track as a crafter. Complete crafting orders and your Moxie increases, unlocking access to better-paying Patron Orders and raising the tips players will offer on commissioned crafts. Abandon or fail orders and your Moxie decreases, locking you out of high-tier work. Moxie is account-wide, so your reputation matters.

🧪 Concentration

A limited account-wide resource that guarantees a craft will produce maximum quality result. Each craft uses one Concentration. You regenerate a small number per day, but can't stockpile more than a few. Use Concentration only on high-value crafts where the quality difference (Rank 4 vs Rank 5) creates massive price variance. Wasting Concentration on low-margin items is a costly mistake.

📜 Treatises

Weekly consumable items that grant +1 Knowledge Point to a specific profession. Every player with crafting professions needs exactly one Treatise per profession per week. Since there are hundreds of crafters per realm all needing them weekly, Treatises have massive consistent demand and are one of the highest-volume items on the AH. Inscription makes them — if you're an Inscriber, this is free money every single day.

📋 Crafting Orders (Work Orders)

Player-to-crafter commissions. Players submit orders with their materials and a tip, you craft the item, they receive it. Public orders are listed for anyone, Personal orders are direct commissions. Higher Artisan Moxie unlocks better-paying Patron Orders from NPCs (guaranteed gold, provided materials). Completing orders successfully builds your reputation; abandoning or failing orders damages it. The system replaced the old crafting quest model.

🔄 Recrafting

Upgrading an existing crafted item to a higher quality tier using the same profession. Instead of crafting brand new gear, players submit their existing item plus materials for upgrade. Recrafting uses fewer materials than crafting from scratch, so players prefer it (cheaper), and crafters profit on the tip while using less resources. It's a lower-barrier entry into crafting orders for new crafters.

💎 Specialization Passives (like Unalloyed Abundance)

Profession-specific perks unlocked by investing Knowledge Points into specialization trees. Examples: Miners get free ore procs from smelting, Alchemists get bonus flask procs, Tailors get bonus cloth. These passives scale with specialization investment — deeper specs = more procs over time. They're a core part of profession profitability and why Knowledge Point allocation matters strategically.

📊 GPH (Gold Per Hour)

The primary metric for measuring gold-making efficiency. Calculate it by dividing total profit by time spent, factoring in AH cut (the 5% fee Blizzard takes from all AH sales). A farming method that generates 1,000,000 gold in 10 hours = 100k GPH. Compare GPH across different methods to optimize where you spend your time. Always include AH cut in calculations or you'll overestimate profits.
